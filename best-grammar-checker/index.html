2. Grammarly — Most Comprehensive

Grammarly is the industry standard. Its AI engine is the most comprehensive on the market, covering grammar, tone, clarity, and plagiarism. But it's expensive and its constant popups can be frustrating.

  • ✅ Best grammar engine on the market
  • ✅ Tone detection, clarity suggestions, plagiarism check

Best for: Academic writers, professionals who need comprehensive reports, people who don't mind the price tag.

3. LanguageTool — Best for Europeans

LanguageTool is open-source and supports 30+ languages, making it great for multilingual writers. But its English grammar engine isn't as strong as Grammarly's or WriteLite's AI.

4. ProWritingAid — Best for Authors

ProWritingAid gives deep reports on pacing, readability, dialogue tags, and style — far more than simple grammar checking. But it's heavy and overkill for daily writing.

5. Hemingway — Best for Readability

Hemingway highlights hard-to-read sentences and passive voice. It's not a grammar checker — it's a readability tool. Great as a supplement, not a replacement.
