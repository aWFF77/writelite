1. Pricing — WriteLite Wins, Big Time

Grammarly Premium starts at $12/month (billed annually) and goes up to $30/month for the business plan. WriteLite is $4.99/month or $39.99/year ($3.33/month).

For the cost of one month of Grammarly Premium, you can use WriteLite for nearly three months. Over a year, WriteLite saves you $100+ compared to Grammarly.

2. Grammar Accuracy — Grammarly Slightly Ahead

Grammarly has been training its AI for 15+ years on billions of documents. WriteLite uses DeepSeek AI, which is excellent for common errors but not as comprehensive for rare edge cases or academic writing.

Verdict: For daily writing (email, Slack, social media), WriteLite catches 90%+ of the errors Grammarly does. For academic papers with complex sentence structures, Grammarly is still the gold standard.

3. User Experience — WriteLite is Way Less Annoying

This is where WriteLite shines. Grammarly is aggressive — popups, upgrade prompts, weekly emails, and a constant red badge showing your "score." WriteLite is quiet by design: it only appears when you ask (triple-space) and disappears after you're done.

"Fix All" button: WriteLite corrects every error with one click. Grammarly requires you to click each suggestion one by one.

4. Privacy — WriteLite is Simpler

WriteLite sends your text to DeepSeek API for grammar checking and does not store or log your text. Grammarly processes everything on its cloud servers and retains documents for your "personal dictionary." If privacy is a priority, WriteLite's approach is cleaner.

Which Should You Choose?

  • Choose WriteLite if: You write emails, messages, and social media posts daily. You want something cheap, quiet, and fast. You're a non-native English speaker who needs quick grammar fixes.
  • Choose Grammarly if: You write long-form documents (essays, reports, books). You need plagiarism checking. You want the most comprehensive grammar engine available. Money is no object.
